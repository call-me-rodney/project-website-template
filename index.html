Blind Aid

A wearable device designed to assist the visually impaired individuals in navigating their surroundings safely using real-time sensor feedback and haptic alert queues.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Individuals with visual impairments often face significant challenges in independently navigating their environments. While some assistive technologies exist, they tend to offer limited feedback about dynamic surroundings and often fail to provide real-time information about nearby obstacles. This lack of awareness can lead to accidents, increased dependence on others, and a reduced sense of confidence and mobility.

We believe that people with visual impairments deserve the freedom to move safely and independently, with greater control over their immediate environment.

SOLUTION

We propose the development of a wearable assistive device that can be easily attached to an individual's clothing. Utilizing a combination of proximity and motion sensors, the device will detect obstacles in real-time and provide tactile feedback to the user. It will also assess the relative speed and distance of approaching obstacles, enabling the user to react accordingly. This system aims to enhance spatial awareness and mobility, empowering visually impaired individuals with greater independence and safety in their daily lives.

The Blind Aid is a wearable device designed to enhance mobility for visually impaired individuals. It uses a combination of sensors (HC-SR04, VL53L0X, MPU6050) to detect obstacles, changes in terrain, and falls, providing real-time haptic feedback. Powered by a 2000mAh Lithium ion battery, it ensures portability and efficiency. Our goal is to make navigation safer and more intuitive, reducing inconvenience for our users.

SYSTEM OVERVIEW

Core Components

A few key componebts that make up our system include:

  • Arduino Uno
  • HC-SR04 Ultrasonic Distance Sensor
  • VL53L0X Time of Flight Sensor
  • PWM Vibration Motor Module
  • MPU6050 Gyroscope and Accelerometer
  • SIM800L EVB Module for SMS alerts
  • Lithium Ion Battery for power supply

PROTOTYPE OVERVIEW

Blind Aid Prototype

Prototype Capabilities

  • Object detection over a 30 degree field of view
  • Complementary sensor system for accurate proximity and speed data at both close and long range
  • Wearer speed and orientation tracking for purposes of fall detection and calculating relative speed
  • An array of vibration queues to convey incoming object speed and proximity
  • SMS Alerts to a victim's caretaker in the event that a fall has been detected.

Protoype Limitations

While developing our protoype, we encountered a number of limitations and hurdles that made the development process challenging.

  • Narrow field of view, thus limited multidirectional object detection
  • Limited sensor range of just over 400 centimetres
  • Constant and accurate calibration of the gyroscope for accurate results
  • Small and ineffiecient power supply
  • SIM800L calibration and integration

FUTURE UPGRADES

With more funding and access to more resources, we have a few system upgrades in mind which include;

  • 90° field of view for multidirectional object detection
  • Increased sensor range up to 600cm
  • GPS tracking
  • 3D route tracing with time, speed and location stamps for improved research and investigative purposes
