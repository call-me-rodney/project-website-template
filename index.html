Blind Aid Version 4

A wearable device designed to assist visually impaired individuals in navigating their surroundings safely using real-time sensor feedback and haptic alerts.

Problem Statement

Individuals with visual impairments often face significant challenges in independently navigating their environments. While some assistive technologies exist, they tend to offer limited feedback about dynamic surroundings and often fail to provide real-time information about nearby obstacles. This lack of awareness can lead to accidents, increased dependence on others, and a reduced sense of confidence and mobility.

We believe that people with visual impairments deserve the freedom to move safely and independently, with greater control over their immediate environment.

Solution

We propose the development of a wearable assistive device that can be easily attached to an individual's clothing. Utilizing a combination of proximity and motion sensors, the device will detect obstacles in real-time and provide tactile or auditory feedback to the user. It will also assess the relative speed and distance of approaching obstacles, enabling the user to react accordingly. This system aims to enhance spatial awareness and mobility, empowering visually impaired individuals with greater independence and safety in their daily lives.

The Blind Aid is a wearable device designed to enhance mobility for visually impaired individuals. It uses a combination of sensors (HC-SR04, VL53L0X, MPU6050) and artificial intelligence to detect obstacles, changes in terrain, and falls, providing real-time audio and haptic feedback. Powered by a 2000mAh Li-Po battery, it ensures portability and efficiency. Our goal is to make navigation safer and more intuitive, reducing inconvenience for users.

Components and their roles:

Arduino Uno

The Arduino Uno serves as the central microcontroller, acting as the brain of the Blind Aid system. It processes real-time data from multiple sensors, including the HC-SR04 and VL53L0X, to detect obstacles and environmental changes. The ATmega328P chip provides 32KB of flash memory and 14 digital I/O pins, enabling efficient control of outputs like the vibration motor. Its low power consumption and versatility make it ideal for wearable applications.

Ultrasonic Distance Sensor (HC-SR04)

The HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor measures distances from 2cm to 4m using sound waves, providing a broad view of the environment. It emits ultrasonic pulses and calculates distance based on the time taken for the echo to return. This sensor is crucial for detecting larger obstacles in the user’s path. Its low cost and wide range make it a reliable choice for initial proximity detection.

Time of Flight Sensor (VL53L0X)

The VL53L0X Time of Flight sensor offers high-precision distance measurements up to 2m, excelling in close-range detection. It uses laser-based technology to detect soft surfaces and narrow objects that the HC-SR04 might miss. Its I2C interface ensures fast data communication with the Arduino Uno. This sensor enhances the system’s ability to provide accurate feedback in complex environments.

PWM Vibration Motor Module

The PWM vibration motor delivers haptic feedback to alert users of obstacles, varying in strength and frequency based on proximity. Controlled via pulse-width modulation (PWM) from the Arduino, it provides intuitive, non-intrusive cues. The motor’s compact size ensures it fits comfortably in the wearable design. Its adjustable intensity helps convey distance and urgency effectively.

MPU6050 (Gyroscope and Accelerometer)

The MPU6050 combines a 3-axis gyroscope and accelerometer to detect orientation and angular velocity, critical for fall detection. It also includes a temperature sensor to adjust HC-SR04 distance calculations for environmental variations. The sensor communicates via I2C, integrating seamlessly with the Arduino. Its high sensitivity ensures accurate detection of sudden movements or falls.

SIM800L EVB

The SIM800L EVB module enables GSM communication to send SMS alerts to caregivers when a fall is detected. It includes proximity data in alerts, providing context for emergencies. The module supports quad-band GSM/GPRS, ensuring reliable connectivity. Its compact design and low power requirements make it suitable for the Blind Aid’s portable system.

Li-Po Battery (2000mAh)

The 2000mAh Li-Po battery provides portable, high-capacity power for the Blind Aid, ensuring extended operation. Its lightweight design is ideal for wearable applications, minimizing user discomfort. The battery supports stable 3.7V output, compatible with the system’s components. It is rechargeable via the TP4056 module for sustained use.

TP4056 Charging Module

The TP4056 module enables safe and efficient charging of the Li-Po battery via micro-USB or USB-C. It provides overcharge and over-discharge protection, extending battery life. The module outputs a stable 3.7V to power the system’s components. Its compact size integrates seamlessly into the wearable design.

MT3608 DC-DC Boost Converter

The MT3608 boost converter steps up the Li-Po battery’s 3.7V to 6V, ensuring stable power for the Arduino and sensors. It supports high efficiency, minimizing energy loss in the wearable system. The adjustable output allows precise voltage tuning for component compatibility. Its small footprint makes it ideal for compact designs.

P-Channel MOSFET (IRF9540N) + Push Button

The P-Channel MOSFET (IRF9540N) and push button enable efficient power management by controlling the system’s on/off state. The MOSFET acts as a switch, reducing power consumption when the device is idle. The push button provides user-friendly manual control. This setup enhances battery life and system reliability.

Prototype Overview

The Blind Aid Version 4 prototype integrates all components into a compact, wearable device, providing real-time navigation assistance through haptic and audio feedback.
